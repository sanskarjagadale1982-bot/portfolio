How I Started Learning Python

When I first started programming, Python was the language that made things feel possible. Its simple and readable syntax helped me focus on solving problems instead of struggling with the language itself. I began with small scripts and gradually moved to building projects like games using Pygame. That’s when I realized coding is not just theory — it’s about creating things that actually work.

Why I Prefer Building Projects Over Just Studying Theory

In my experience, tutorials and theory are helpful, but real learning happens when you build something on your own. While working on my portfolio and Python projects, I faced many bugs and errors. Each mistake forced me to understand concepts more deeply. These challenges improved my problem-solving skills and made me more confident as a developer.

Why Full Stack Development Excites Me

Full stack development connects everything — frontend, backend, and databases. I enjoy working on both the user interface and the logic behind it. It gives me a complete understanding of how modern applications work. Building full projects from scratch is something I find both challenging and exciting.
